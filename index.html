Leicester

A city in England’s East Midlands region

Leicester: historic, diverse, Roman roots, King Richard III, Premier League glory.

Leicester is a city in England’s East Midlands region. Leicester Cathedral, where Richard III was reinterred in 2015, has stood at the city’s heart for over 900 years. Close by, the King Richard III Visitor Centre tells the story of the king’s life and death and displays his original burial site. The ruins of Leicester Castle, where Richard III spent some of his last days, lie in Castle Gardens, near the River Soar.

Facts About Leicester

1. Historical Roots

Leicester is one of England's oldest cities, with a history that dates back over 2,000 years. It was originally founded as a Roman settlement called Ratae Corieltauvorum around AD 47.

2.King Richard III's Resting Place

Leicester gained international attention in 2012 when the remains of King Richard III were discovered beneath a car park. He was reburied in Leicester Cathedral in 2015.

3.Cultural Diversity

Leicester is one of the UK's most ethnically diverse cities. It's known for its vibrant multicultural festivals, including Diwali celebrations, which are among the largest outside India.

4.Sporting Glory

Leicester City Football Club made history in 2016 by winning the Premier League against all odds. The triumph is considered one of the greatest achievements in football history.

5. Food and Drink

Leicester is famous for its food scene, especially its contributions to British cuisine. The city is home to the iconic Walkers Crisps brand and is renowned for its authentic Indian cuisine.
